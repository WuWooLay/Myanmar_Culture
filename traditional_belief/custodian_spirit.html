Custodian Spirit

Written by U Than Pe ( Tour Guide )
Custodian Spirit

Myanmars believe in rebirth and next life . We know nothing is permanent in this impermanent world , anicca . Still some of us do forget and accumulate so much worldly possessions especially gold , silver and jewellery we cannot use up in our life time . What do we do with them ?Most of the lot we bury them , in a secret place . The whole operation of burying is carried out secretly . So secretly that not even wife and children know , where the treasure lies . Well when miserly owner dies his attachment to his board makes him . Ok-da-zaunt a custodian spirit of the treasure trove.

Our belied in custodian spirit of the treasure adds some words to our vocabulary and also produce a separate class of treasure hunters . They are called thike saya . They collect local literature called thike tsa the coded descriptions of ways and means to recover the treasure trove . The codes contain do's and don'ts for the treasure hunter and party . Since the message is in code skill , experience acumen is needed for interpretation . Misinterpretation of code will render the hunt fruitless and sometimes even land the whole party in trouble.

One story is told about one treasure hunting party . They found an instruction mee ma taung ya don't ask for the light for your smoking . But if you reverse the instruction it will mean maun ma tee ya,gong should not be beaten . As a fact there appeared a gong and a stick on recitation of special coded mantra . So , one of the party , naturally picked up the gong and beat it . Lo! No sooner has the sound of gong was heard that the whole scene changed from arid lands of central Myanmar to a shallow lake of clear blue water . The whole party had to leave their necromanic parapherenalia in the flood and swim for their lives . That was until a farmer called them asking what they were doing with their belly on the ground . Then and there their feet touched the hard ground . With their knees and elbows bruised from crawling on the hard soil , they stood up sheepishly.

Custodian spirits are also depicted as brother and sister , husband and wife . With one taking a leave of certain years , say 20 , from the other and being reborn as an ordinary human lady or gentleman . They are usually beautiful or handsome as the case may be . The person himself or herself believe it and he or she usually die at the end of stipulated number of years . They usually fall in love while they are human beings . They give some treasure or heat-break usually the later when the time comes for their return to the spirit life . If the rebirth is known in time , their return can be prevented.

It also offers unscrupulous person to claim that their ill-gotten gains are in fact the gift of the custodian spirit . The statement may not satisfy the tax collector but it convinces the local population and establish him as an honest person . They don't see him as a person who dipped his fingers into the public till.

The belief in custodian spirit produced some colorful vocabulary in our Bama language . Thike thamar means an unscrupulous Romeo wooing a rich spinster . Ba-na-phu thaik too thu means amoral opportunist . Thattu thaik means rich mineral deposits . Many pictures were made before early 60s based on wellknown treasure troves , in Bama folklore . They were marvelous money-spinners . They preceded Harry Potter by more than half a century.
