Panca Rupa (Pyin-Sa-Ru-Pa)

Written by U Than Pe ( Tour Guide )
Panca Rupa

In the days gone by there was no atomic power , no supersonic jets , no television , no computers . The life was simple and predictable for our ancestors with only some occasional witch-craft and magic to disrupt the monotony of existance. But they were very clever in creating fables with fabulous power. They created legends which are now our cultural heritage . One of them is none other than the "Panca rupa " pronounced (pyin-sa-ru-pa) by most of our people .

It is an imaginary animal created out of different organs from different animals It is a composite creature . It has the tusks and trunk of an elephant , the most powerful land animal . It has four legs of a horse , the most useful , clever and swift domesticated animal . It is given power to fly with the wing of a bird . Water of moats , ditches or rivers might not be able to block its path it is provided with a carp's tail: very useful for swimming across watery blockades .Horn of "toe-n,yaa" adds more offensive power to its already formidable strength and arsenal .

The figure of panca-rupa is a must for full-scale Myanmar orchestra . It is gilded and acts as a cross-bar bearing the weight of big drum . It also adorn columns and pillars in the vicinity of many a stupa or temple .

Many bells large and small have their top loops adoorned with the figure of a pance-rupa . Evan in monastic compounds and gates one can sometimes see panca-rupas painted , sculptured or carved as a relief . It has really become one of the most important and everpresent Myanmar mythical creatures .

Well "toe-n,yaa" is another mythical creature which adorns many gold coins hung around Myanmar toddlers' necks . It has been around for centuries among Myanmars . It contributed horns to panca-rupa.
