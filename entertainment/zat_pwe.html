Zat Pwe

Written by U Than Pe ( Tour Guide )

Myanmar are happy people . Whatever the social occasion they will have a celebration to fit it . Birth , baptism , naming , novitiation , earboring , wedding all call for a celebration . Site clearing , stake driving , ground breaking , post erecting , beam hoisting , root thatching , house warming each demands a celebration as elaborate as their purse allows . For such jolly people auspicious religious day are godsend . On top of it our people have inventive mind . They white-wash a locally well known pagoda . They gold leaf its umbrella and hold a festival in its dedication . On its anniversary they hold another festival . After a few years' repetitions the new pagoda festival becomes well established . A few decades later few remember how it all started but it proudly takes its place on the regional calendar . This is our age-old tradition.

Zat Pwe

Until dethroning of our last King and colonization royal patronage supported our artistes like dancers , singers and musicians . Colonialism changed the social structure . Royal entertainers organized themselves into zat ahphwes and started entertaining town dwellers and rural folks.

As first they performed free of charge but welcomed contributions from spectator-audience and local dignitaries . Their stage was usually a clearing at the foot of a tree . Beside the tree they planted a leafy branch . It was meant to suggest a forest . The central clearing served as a stage . The large wooden box containing their stage dresses ; now empty , served as a throne . Musicians with their instruments occupied the edge of the stage . Stage light was provided by a couple of kerosine or petrol gas lamps hung from the branch.

Publicity for the performance was through ywazawa the village heralds . They carried a small brass circular gong . They stood at the gate of the village , beat the gong as many time as they fancy , to get the attention of village populace . Once they get the attention they shout the message in the words they deem to be proper . Usually they tell the people , the place , the cast list headed by the famous actor and actress . The name of the music band , the saing ahphwe is an important draw.

For audience ? They had to bring their own mat and oil lamps or kerosine lamps , and of course , every thing they needed as snack . As the time is usually early or middle winter they have to bring some light blankets . They carry rolls of mats on their head if they are from the nearby villages . If they are from far away village they come by bullock carts.

Audience surround the stage . They unroll their mats and spread on the ground . Aisle is usually marked by two parallel lines marked on the ground with unslaked lime powder . Just outside the area occupied by mats bullock carts are parked parallel to one another . Cart folk watch the performance from their parked carts, their magnificent grandstand.

The story performed on the stage is always connected with Lord Buddha's previous births , ten jataka stories . They are Zat Taws , all Buddhists in those days knew them by heart . Some could even recite the pali passages . No one forgets te,za,thu,nay,ma,bu,tsan,na,vi,vay . There are some exceptions , like , Min Kusa- PaPa Wady , Sadan SinMinn , Thuhtithena-Thambula .

Zat pwe stared about an hour after sundown . It went on until dawn . Nothing is scripted every actor knew his or her line . Some of them were very smart they could put the words into averse. The language of the stage was royal and official and reflected the glories of bygone Myanmar Kings and dynasties . They hadn't forgotten their days at court.

From those humble beginnings Myanmar ZatThabin developed and shone brilliantly on the stage for half a century reaching its zenith between World War . First half half the last century saw the great Zat Ahphwes always connected with the lead actor's name . Pho Sein Gyi , Sein Aung Min , Shweman Tin Maung , U Aung Maung Shwenan Tin and many others....they all rush into my mind bringing tears of nostalgia . I feel sorry I cannot do any justice to their memory . The list is not complete norchronological.

Soon after independence , authorities establish a school for performing arts . The Pantra Kyaung . It tried to revive the declining art and produced some artistes. One of the school's students dizzied the theatre world , as Pantra Kyi Lin.

But first competitor was cinema . At first cinema was silent with actors and their dialogues appearing alternately on the screen . Unit then Myanmar Zat Thabin was able to hold its own as it was a level playing field . Talkies raised the stakes and the playing field tilted against it . Black and White gave way to Technicolour , Gevacolour films . But our Zat Thabin carried on against great odds . But arrival of CD,DVD finally pushed it off the stage . Now it is a relic of the past glory.

Now authorities have started state competition and a national level soe-ka-yay-tee singing , dancing , writing and playing musical instruments every year . It is a laudable project . Whether it can revive Myanmar ZatThabin is immaterial . It can surely preserve our priceless national heritage for future generations.

What I have given above is one person's view based on my own experience and recollections . Old people born under King Mindon's region used to sit and reminisce about their golden past . In the idle afternoons on a roofless serai under an old yellow-bark acacia at the centre of our village I used to listen to their talks . This is not a treatise nor a thesis . Its given here for younger generation's food for thought and for improving their insight, into our golden past . I want them to take it in the spirit it is given.
