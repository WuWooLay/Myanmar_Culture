Neem

Written by U Than Pe ( Tour Guide )
Neem

The person who meets the visitors at the point of entry to a country or a region is a guide . Under normal conditions the person who see off visitors at the point of departure is also guide . There are of course, mean spirited tour companies that do not allow the guide to see off the visitors . They replace the guide with nephew or niece . Such companies are not few nor far between . They are substantial ; no way out . For guides have to sell their services to the lowest bidder--it is byer's market.

Now let's have a look at the day's work of a guide . She or he goes to the company . Takes the list containing names of visitors from the company desk : Goes to airport. Holds a placard with the visitor's name and wait at the arrival lounge . Greets the visitors , checks the list , leads them to the waiting bus . Rechecks the list in the bus. Then starts his or her commentary on the bus . The voice booms out with the opening phrase of each sentence--"Look to the left--Look to the right--".So it goes on until the bus arrives at the hotel.

At the hotel, if there are a large number of visitors in the group the guide has to help the receptionist to register them . As very good guide knows a photocopy of the list is very helpful . It saves time and breath . Some guide have written to me asking for ready to use commentaries . As so two situation can be identical it will be inappropriate to give such pieces . But this article will give some ideas to building or aspiring guides . For a general reader it will give some insights. There were six tourists . Four of them were restaurant workers . They always wanted to try a local dish . They had been through the three countries of Indian sub-continent In Monywa , we went into Myanmar dishes for them that were locally popular.

For the driver , helper and myself , I ordered tomatoes in oil , cooked with Mergui dried fish . Salad to go was tender neem leaf boiled with plum . Soup was green lablab beans with acacia intsia.All of the visitors were intrigued by the dishes on our table . Each of them took some boiled neem and chewed it . Animated talk issued . It offered a great chance for a long presentation.

After lunch we started north . We stopped on the Ahlone road between Dawnagyan and Yongyee . It was under an old massive neem tree . I started " Ladies and gentlemen, the bitter leaf you sampled from our table comes from these trees . They are called neem trees . There are three varieties in Myanmar . Once is called Bama neem . It is preferred as salad in up country . The second is Kalaneem . It is used as toothbrush by Indian Buddhist Sanghas . They take a twig and chew its one end into a brush . Every morning each new fresh twig is used . In lower Myanmar both varieties are eaten . Third variety is called flowering neem . It is never eaten". Their bark gave reddish brown color to cotton yarn in olden days . Their leaves and bark were boiled , in big earthen pots or small drums . That water was the best and only treatment for scabies during Japaneses occupation and few years beyond . Neem seed contains a goodly amount of oil . Neem oil is produced on commercial scale in Magwe division . It is used in antiseptic soap and some medicines . Its leaves,when tender , are boiled with ripe or green tamarind beans or plum berries and eaten as salad . Resin from neem tree is traditionally used as glue."

Neem fruit , leaf , bark , root , even its wood ,are bitter . Whenever Myanmars want to compare they use neem as a standard bearer for bitter . The voice of the person you hate is said to be as bitter as neem no matter which part as all its parts are bitter . Comparison sounds negative but the idea is never negative . It borders on reverant . Neem has become part of Myanmar culture and literature."
