Plain Tea

Written by U Than Pe ( Tour Guide )
Plain Tea

Visitors to up country Myanmar villages are always intrigued by earthen kettles . They are round bottomed complete with lid spout and handle . They are always black with soot , as they are used for boiling water as well as serving plain tea . Tourists buy those earthen kettle souvenirs . These humble kettle are still used in almost every house in a village . They have served village folk for time immemorial .

Plain tea is the most common term . Westerners call it green tea or Chinese tea . Green tea is of course , a misnomer . The tea leaf is neither green by color , it is usually black . Nor it is green by state , it is totally dry . Yes , it may be call Chinese tea . Use of tea in China and the other Far Eastern countries is a well documented history . Chinese enjoy aroma of tea . Japanese have tea ceremony . But Myanmar take tea for its bitter-astringent taste . Aroma is taken as bonus.

According to one legend tea came to China via Tartars . Tartars invaded China . They moved fast across vast plains of central Asia . They met different taste of water almost everyday . So to make the taste uniform they boiled the local water . Boiled water teste the same everyday . But its bland taste is unappealing . So they added some leaves or flowers or even roots to the boiling water . That made the boiled water aromatic and gave it a mild taste . That is the legend.

In Myanmar tea is grown in hilly regions . Namsan , Koekang , Keng Tung , Pindaya , Thandaung and many other area grow quality tea . Tea picking is very labour intensive process . A lot of seasonal labour from Mandalay and Sagaing divisions move to hilly regions of Navsam . Green , tender , tea leaves are picked by hand and cured . Finally it is dried and packed.

Myanmar boil water and throw in a pinch or a fistful of tea leaf the boiling kettle . They let it brew for some time of the stove . Then they serve it in various ways . Some to quench their thirst . In hot weather when one comes from walk or work under the sun the preferred drink is a hot cup of plain tea because it is believed that cool or cold water can even bring on a heat stroke.

In every house there will be a kettle and cups , may be also an enamel ware tray , for serving plain tea . In towns people keep thermos full of plain tea . There may be tealeaf salad or ginger-slice salad to go with plain tea . Some keep , toddy sugar candy called jaggery , ground nut brittle , vegetable marrow candy , depending on the means of the host . Some may flay a quick omelette with sugar or vegetable for a chance visitor . Some families use plain tea in lieu of soup at dinner table .

Farm labours, paid or volunteer carpenters , are liberally supplied with pots of plain tea . In fact woodstove is built with a few bricks beside the worksite . The hot water kettle is kept boiling all the time . At the end of the meal plain tea is a must . No meal is considered complete without a pot or a thermos of plain tea at the end .

Chinese people take many kinds of flower , leaves roots and even barks to make tea . But Myanmar stick to only two kind of tea . Tea from camelia drupifera , a small tea tree and camelia theifera a shrub call tea plant . These tea varieties yield the best for Myanmar plate . Tea leaves after serving as plain tea are thrown out . Tealeaf salad is not made from the used tea . It is prepared differently at source . There is a large tree called yellow bark acacia(Acacia leucopploea) , hta-naung in local parlence in up country . Its leaves are used for brewing plain tea . They are also used as salad . People believe That those leaves increase and facilitate the flow of urine . It is widely eaten in early and midsummer . Whatever the variety , plain tea is part of Myanmar culture . It has been so for centuries . Ancient tea kettles unearthed by archaeologists prove that.
