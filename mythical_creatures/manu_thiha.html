Manu-Thiha

Written by U Than Pe ( Tour Guide )
Manu Thiha

In the vicinity of many stupas and temples somewhere on the terace or plinth we can find very strange figures of creatures . They usually stand at the corners with two bodies joined at the shoulders; one body on each side with human head and neck resting on a human shoulder joining the two lion bodies in perfect symmetry . The creature is known as manu-thiha . In sanskrit vocabulary manu means human and thiha mean lion:man-lion a vary apt name of course .

Many legends surround this manu-thiha and its origin . One of most quoted is the one with its connections to the ancient kingdom of Suvana-bhumi now called Thaton . According to that legend King's children were always devoured by an ogress and her one and a half thousand attends on the day they were born . This tragedy went on for some time .

But one day Buddhist missionary party of two Bikkhus Shinsoka and Shin-ottra appeared on the scene . That day happened to be the day on which queen was expecting a son . The Buddhist bikkus created three thousand strange creatures with human-head and twin bodies of lions . When ogress and her attendants saw those creatures they ran back to their base in the sea under water : needless to say ogress and her attendants never returned to threaten the new born prince again .

The child so saved became king in due course of time . He ordered the whole nation to draw the likeness of these manu-thiha on palm leaves and hang around the necks of their children to ward of the danger of the aggressive ogress . With passing of time the creature found its way on to the terraces of temples and stupas .
